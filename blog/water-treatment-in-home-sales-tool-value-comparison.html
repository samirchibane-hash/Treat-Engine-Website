The kitchen-table moment where deals are won or lost

Every water treatment sale comes down to one number the homeowner is running in their head: "Is this actually worth it?"

They're not rejecting your system. They're rejecting a price tag they can't yet connect to a payoff. The hard water is invisible. So is the money it quietly costs them — every year, whether they buy from you or not.

That gap between price today and value over 20 years is where in-home sales stall. It's exactly what the Value Comparison step (Step 6) of the ClearDeals New Proposal wizard was built to close.

If you're a dealership owner, this is about a more predictable close rate across your whole team. If you're a sales rep, it's about walking out with a signature tonight instead of a "let me think about it." Same feature, both jobs — so let's talk to both.

Why "trust me, it pays off" doesn't sell anymore

Homeowners are more skeptical and more informed than they were five years ago. A verbal claim — "you'll save thousands over the life of the system" — lands as a sales line, not a fact.

The best water treatment in-home sales tool doesn't ask the customer to take your word for it. It shows them, on screen, in their own home, with their numbers. That's the shift the Value Comparison step makes: it turns your value story from something the rep says into something the customer sees.

What the Value Comparison step actually shows

It's the sixth step of the wizard — landing right after you've built the system and set maintenance, and right before Terms & Agreement. That placement is deliberate: the customer sees the payoff at the exact moment before the ask.

On one screen, the homeowner sees:

  • The headline number: "Best Net Savings Over 20 Years." In the walkthrough above, that's $16,582 in savings — against $39,600 spent doing nothing. The comparison isn't "buy vs. don't buy." It's "get ahead vs. keep bleeding money."
  • Three cumulative-cost lines side by side: Current Spending (the status quo), Pay In Full, and Finance Plan. The customer instantly sees how each path costs out over time.
  • The break-even dot on each line — the point "where the system has paid for itself." After that dot, the system isn't an expense anymore. It's a return.
  • The crossover where the cost of doing nothing climbs past the cost of owning the system — the visual "aha" that reframes the whole conversation.

Instead of a price that feels like a wall, the customer sees a line that bends in their favor. "It's expensive" becomes "it's expensive not to."

For the sales rep: your close rate lives in this step

It does the math so you don't have to defend it. No more scribbling savings on a napkin the customer quietly distrusts. Step 6 renders it cleanly — you point, they lean in.

It reframes financing instantly. Because Finance Plan sits right next to Current Spending, the customer sees the monthly payment measured against what they're already losing every month. When the payment lands under that line, "yes" is the obvious move.

The break-even dot kills the stall. "Let me think about it" is usually uncertainty, not rejection. A concrete point where the system pays for itself removes the fog that makes people postpone — and decisions get made in the room, the only place in-home reps get paid.

It makes you the advisor, not the pitchman. Walking a homeowner through Pay-in-Full vs. Finance vs. Doing Nothing positions you as an expert helping them choose — not a salesperson pushing one option.

For the dealership owner: consistency is the real ROI

It standardizes your best pitch. Your top closer's instinct for framing 20-year value is now a fixed step in the wizard. Your newest rep tells the same story on their first appointment. You raise the floor of the whole team, not just the ceiling.

It shortens ramp time. New hires close sooner when the tool carries the value argument — less dependence on tenure and natural talent.

It protects margin. A rep who can show a $16,582 net-savings figure discounts less. You defend price with a chart instead of caving on it.

It lifts close rate without adding lead cost. Every point of conversion from the same appointments is pure margin. A better water treatment in-home sales tool is the cheapest growth lever you have.

Built into the flow reps already use

This isn't a separate app, a spreadsheet, or a PDF someone forgets to bring. Value Comparison is Step 6 of the New Proposal wizard your reps already run on every appointment:

  • Build the system and set maintenance in the earlier steps.
  • The 20-year projection and net-savings figure generate automatically from those inputs.
  • Present it, then move straight into Terms & Agreement (Step 7) and signature — without ever leaving ClearDeals.

The value story and the close live in the same flow. That's exactly why it converts.

From invisible value to undeniable value

Water treatment has always had a value problem, not a product problem. The systems perform. The savings are real. The only challenge is making the customer see it before they talk themselves out of the purchase.

The Value Comparison step is how ClearDeals solves that — turning your strongest argument into a picture the homeowner can't unsee. For the rep, more signatures tonight. For the owner, a repeatable close across the team.
